Design a modern, high-quality waitlist landing page website for a product called Nibbl.
This is NOT a generic startup page. It should feel like a well-crafted product experience designed by a strong product designer in 2026. Avoid anything that looks templated, overly symmetric, or “AI generated.” The layout should feel intentional, slightly asymmetrical where needed, and visually balanced.
________________


PRODUCT CONTEXT
Nibbl is a food discovery platform, not a delivery app.
It helps users discover great food spots around them based on:
* mood and cravings
* budget
* dietary preferences (veg, non-veg, vegan, high protein)
* hyperlocal exploration
* hidden gems (street food, tapris, small shops)
It also includes:
* bucket lists (track food spots visited)
* bookmarks / saved lists
* curated area lists (e.g., best spots in Bandra)
* shareable lists
The core idea is:
“Discover food worth stepping out for.”
________________


DESIGN DIRECTION (VERY IMPORTANT)
The design should feel:
* modern, clean, and editorial
* slightly experimental but still usable
* inspired by high-end product websites, not SaaS templates
* immersive and visual (food, maps, exploration)
* tactile and layered (depth, shadows, motion hints)
Avoid:
* flat boring sections
* generic gradients everywhere
* stock UI blocks
* overuse of cards
Use:
* large typography
* strong hierarchy
* whitespace with intention
* subtle motion cues (hover, parallax feel)
* layered compositions (map + food + UI elements overlapping slightly)
The page should feel like a product you want to explore, not just read.
________________


PAGE STRUCTURE
1. HERO SECTION
Goal: instantly communicate the product.
Content:
Title:
“Discover the best food spots around you.”
Subtext:
Explore hidden gems, street food, cafes, and local favorites based on your mood, budget, and location.
Primary CTA:
“Join the waitlist”
Secondary CTA (optional):
“Explore how it works”
Visual direction:
* A dynamic map-based background with floating food markers
* Subtle UI preview of the app (not full mockups, just glimpses)
* Depth with overlapping layers
* Slight motion feel (like things are alive, not static)
________________


2. PROBLEM SECTION
Goal: make users feel the pain.
Heading:
“Finding good food is harder than it should be.”
Content blocks (not bullet heavy, more visual storytelling):
* cluttered map results
* unreliable reviews
* missing hidden gems
* asking friends repeatedly
Visual:
* messy map vs clean discovery contrast
* storytelling through layout, not paragraphs
________________


3. SHIFT IN THINKING
Heading:
“People don’t search restaurants. They search cravings.”
Show:
* cheap eats
* late night food
* high protein meals
* chai tapri
* date spots
Visual:
* floating search queries or tags
* dynamic arrangement (not rigid grid)
________________


4. PRODUCT INTRO
Heading:
“Meet Nibbl”
Short explanation:
Nibbl helps you discover food based on how you feel, where you are, and what you want to eat.
Visual:
* app preview (map + list + filters)
* should feel real, not placeholder
________________


5. CORE FEATURES (INTERACTIVE FEEL)
Break into 4–5 sections, each with strong visual + minimal text.
A. Mood-Based Discovery
B. Hidden Gems & Street Food
C. Hyperlocal Exploration
D. Bucket Lists & Tracking
E. Save & Share Lists
Each section:
* should feel slightly different in layout
* include micro-interaction hints (hover states, transitions)
* avoid repetitive card layouts
________________


6. BUCKET LIST FEATURE (IMPORTANT)
Heading:
“Turn food into experiences.”
Show:
* checklist UI
* visited vs not visited
* progress indicator
Make it feel satisfying and gamified.
________________


7. SAVE & ORGANIZE
Heading:
“Save now. Explore later.”
Show collections like:
* Late Night Food
* Cheap Eats
* Cafes
* Street Food
Visual style:
* layered boards or stacked layouts
* not a simple grid
________________


8. SHAREABLE LISTS
Heading:
“Food is better when shared.”
Show:
* list preview
* share interaction
* social context
Make it feel social but not like a social media app.
________________


9. SOCIAL PROOF / EARLY INTEREST
Even if fake for now, design space for:
* “Join 500+ early users”
* testimonials (optional placeholders)
Keep subtle, not loud.
________________


10. FINAL CTA
Heading:
“Never wonder where to eat again.”
CTA:
Join the waitlist
Optional microcopy:
Be the first to discover your city through food.
________________


11. FOOTER
Simple but clean:
* product name
* short description
* social links placeholder
* email
________________


INTERACTIONS & EDGE CASES (VERY IMPORTANT)
Design all states, not just static screens.
Include:
WAITLIST FORM STATES
* default
* typing
* success state
* error state (invalid email)
* loading state
CTA BUTTON STATES
* hover
* pressed
* disabled
EMPTY STATES
* no content
* loading placeholders
RESPONSIVENESS
* desktop (primary)
* tablet
* mobile
Mobile should NOT feel like a squished desktop.
Reflow layouts intentionally.
SCROLL EXPERIENCE
* sections should transition smoothly
* slight parallax or depth shifts
* avoid abrupt stacking
MICRO INTERACTIONS
* hover on cards
* subtle movement on visuals
* feedback on clicks
________________


VISUAL LANGUAGE
* Use real-looking food imagery (not overly polished stock)
* Combine maps + UI + photography
* Maintain strong contrast and readability
* Avoid loud or messy color usage
* Keep it premium and intentional
________________


FINAL OUTPUT EXPECTATION
The result should feel like:
* a real startup landing page ready to launch
* designed by a human with taste
* visually rich but not overwhelming
* clearly communicating the product
It should NOT feel like:
* a template
* a generic SaaS landing page
* a quick AI-generated layout
Focus on clarity, depth, and craft.
